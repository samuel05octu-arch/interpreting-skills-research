Section III

Problem Formulation

Consider the previous pilot survey conducted by research group. The following research question has been formulated according to the findings in the perception pilot survey: How can Uniminuto University support students in developing skills to become a French interpreter?

Taking into account this problem question, the principal challenges are that the Uniminuto University does not offer a specific subject to improve these communicative skills in French, primarily because the program is focused on teacher education, and the French subjects go up until B2; Moreover there is not a specific subject with the objective of strengthen interpreting skills.

There is an academic gap between the skills required for professional interpretation and the current program offered to French students at the B2 level.

The findings strongly supported that some students are interested in developing as interpreters. Although achieving a B2 level in French shows control of the idiom does not imply professional interpreting competence. According to the Council of Europe (2001). B2 learner is able to understand the main ideas of complex texts, and interact with native speakers. However interpretation skills concern additional abilities.

Albl-Mikasa (2013) argues that not everybody can become an interpreter because developing interpreter competences are (linguistic) knowledge of their mother tongue and foreign language, the technicalities of interpreting proper, and business-related know-how. This approach demonstrates that achieving a B2 french level is not a guarantee of possessing the necessary competences.

The analysis confirms that is necessary to examine how the Uniminuto university can implement academic strategies that allow the strengthening of interpretation competences of the interested students interested in pursuing this professional path.

Research Objectives

The following objectives delineate the scope and purpose of the proposed pedagogical intervention within the Foreign Languages Bachelor's degree framework.

01.

General objective

Propose a French Interpreter Training Camp for developing interpretation skills for B2 French learners in the Foreign Languages bachelor's degree program at Minuto de Dios University during the 2025-2 semester.

02.

Specific objectives

Identify the intercultural and soft communication skills necessary for effective interpretation in the French language.

Explore strategies for creating a syllabus that utilizes the identified communication skills to enhance the foreign language abilities of intermediate-level students.

Analyze the appropriate methodologies for developing these skills.

Literature Review

My research investigates which skills are necessary to become as a French interpreter for a group of B2 french learners in a bachelor degree of uniminuto university. That plans to propuse a didactic unit to the University for B2 french learners. In the following lines there is a revision of 5 articles related to it, organized depending on the year of chronological order.

Beeby, A., et al (2005) was focused un an experiment on translation competence. Its experiment was comparing expert translators with non-translator language teachers, highlighting differences in the use of cognitive strategies (internal support) and documentary resources (external support). This study is relevant to mine because of the translation and for extension the interpretation. Their findings show that having a good French level for example b2 in uniminuto students do not guarantee the capacity for made interpretation. Because this competence require specific subcompetences that they must develop in specialized training

Schimke, et al (2018) examined first language influence on second language interpretation. In this experimental quantitative study, the participants were students of French and German language, their first language was Spanish. The first experiment provides new data for Spanish as first language. In second experiment, it assessed offline and online interpretations preferences for French and Spanish of German speakers as their first language. Despite this study compared others languages Its methodology also provides me a reference for designing practical strategies in my study.

In 2019 Alcalde Peñalver, et al. Based his study in training prospective legal practitioners to work with translators and interpreters. In this qualitative study results revealed low levels of familiarization with the translation profession among law students and they did not even know about the regulations that govern their work. His study, however, was limited, because was focused just in how law students was familiarized with interpreters, and his findings are not enough helpful for mine. Despite it demonstrates that interpreters require more than language knowledge; Meaning that becoming a French interpreter involves not only mastering the language but also developing well-defined skills.

Communities of practice support language proficiency, intercultural awareness, and collaboration were studied by Kaszuba, et al (2024). Those aspects are also key for interpretation training. That contrast with mine because it highlights the value of structured support in developing communication skills for French learners. their findings are not specific enough to directly support my research. The study would have been more relevant if it had explored the specific skills necessary to become an interpreter, which is the main focus of my investigation.

Garrett, B. D., et al (2025). Examined how the Current State of ASL-English Interpreting Education Across the United States. The authors analyzed publicly available website data to document the number and type of active postsecondary interpreter education programs by state. Despite this study was focused in Interpreting Education, its findings are not helpful for mine, taking into account that my main idea is necessaries skills to become as a French interpreter However their findings would have been more useful if it had a experiment about skills and challenges that students face it.

The fist key area of Yenkimaleki, M., et al. (2025). Is about the effect of fluency training on interpreter trainees' speech fluency, comprehensibility, and accentedness: This experiment was set up to investigate the effect of fluency training on speech fluency, comprehensibility, and accentedness of interpreter trainees. The significant findings revealed that the fluency training significantly enhanced the interpreter trainees' fluency, and to a lesser extent the students' comprehensibility but had only a marginal effect on accentedness. The findings are really useful for mine because taking into account that fluency, comprehensibility, and accentedness are key parameters of interpreting quality. It shows that targeted fluency training significantly improves interpreter trainees’ performance.

Gaur, V. (2025). Created an quantitative methodology for know how the hard skills and soft skills can affect the innovation capacity and development of university teachers. This in fact is partially useful for my research as it strengthens the professional development, in this situation the interpretation. Their findings can be used analogously in my research because this study was focused on urban technological innovation rather than language training or interpretation, its central conclusion that global and local are mutually reinforcing.

Yenkimaleki, M., et al (2023) Examine the effects of prosody instruction by carrying out two experiments with various approaches. Their findings suggest the value of combining human feedback and clear explanations in the development of critical auditory skills for interpretation. These findings could have a big impact on my research because they show how crucial oral comprehension is, even at the B2 level, and how it forms the foundation for interpretation. However, if the study had concentrated on interpretation learners, it would have been more beneficial.

Theoretical Framework

Keywords: ICC, Balboni, Interculturalism, Communication Models

1. The need for a model of intercultural communicative competence

According to Balboni (2006), communication between two people from different cultures does not depend only on linguistic competence. For him, language is only one of the different elements that take part in the interaction. Communication is a wide process where different aspects participate, such as social expectations and cultural features, that shape the way in which people interpret messages.

Keeping this in mind, Balboni proposes a detailed model of intercultural communicative competence, ICC, that helps explain how communication is in different contexts. The main idea of the author is that language and culture cannot be separated. Learning to communicate in another language is also understanding the cultural systems that give it shape. If the learner does not understand the cultural elements that affect communication, it is very possible that the messages are interpreted in a wrong way, even when the linguistic level is good.

2. The concept of culture according to Balboni

The concept presents a wide understanding of culture. Balboni explains that culture is a system of meanings that affects the way of speaking and the way of seeing reality. Culture, according to Balboni, is not only traditions or fixed facts, but an internal software that guides human behavior.

Culture participates in communication through the perception of time, social rules, family and professional roles, emotional expression, use of personal space, correct use of politeness, interpretation of authority, and expectations about behavior. All these aspects affect the way people communicate.

3. The ICC model of Balboni: general structure

The main contribution of the book is the model of Intercultural Communicative Competence (ICC). Balboni organizes this model in three systems that work like softwares that regulate intercultural communication:

  • Software of the mind
  • Software of communication
  • Software of context

3.1. Software of the mind

The software of the mind refers to the cultural values that people, members of a community, have learned. Balboni describes these values as deep patterns that guide behavior without people noticing them. Some examples are: the cultural concept of time (punctuality or flexibility), social organization and hierarchy, the relationship with older people or authority figures, the importance of family, rules of respect, politeness and interpersonal treatment, and ideas about work, cooperation or competition.

3.2. Software of communication

This system includes the codes used to transmit messages.

Verbal code: It includes the structure of the discourse, the use of vocabulary, grammatical forms, the phonological system, and pragmatic rules. The author explains that these elements have a cultural component. For example, some cultures prefer direct discourse and others prefer indirect discourse. In some cultures, silence can be respect, and in others, it can show disagreement.

Non-verbal code: The author says that non-verbal communication has an important role in cultural interaction. This includes gestures, body posture, physical distance, eye contact, facial movements, volume and rhythm of the voice, and also personal appearance. Every culture uses and understands these elements in different ways. For this reason, intercultural competence also means learning to read these codes with a deep level of detail, similar to the analysis of verbal language.

3.3. Software of context

The third software is related to the context where communication happens. The author uses communicative events, which are specific situations that have expectations and rules. These events include formal presentations, speeches, daily conversations, negotiations, professional meetings, classes, group interactions, and institutional exchanges.

4. Intercultural competence as a skill that develops

Balboni explains that intercultural competence is not learned only in a theoretical way. It is developed through practice, observation, experience, comparison, and reflection. Learning happens when the student observes, identifies, shares, reflects, and adapts his or her behavior according to the context. To reach a high intercultural competence means a deep change in the way the student sees communication. It stops being only a linguistic activity and becomes an interpretative process that combines language, culture, and behavior.

5. Application of the ICC model to language learning and interpretation

Even if the book is not directed only to interpretation, what we can analyze can be applied, because interpreting is understanding a message and transmitting it in the same or a similar way. And this is possible if the student or the interpreter dominates the three softwares described by Balboni. The software of the mind helps the student understand the culture inside the message. The software of communication helps to understand the verbal and non-verbal codes used by the speaker. And the software of context guides the way to adapt a discourse.

Research Design

Research Design This research focuses on the development of the necessary communicative skills in intermediate-level French students. This research aims to analyze the necessary skills to become a French interpreter and propose an interpretation trainer club designed to strengthen those skills. Therefore, it is necessary to follow a constructivist paradigm because this allows understanding participants’ experiences and interpreting their learning process. Taking this into account, the approach used is going to be qualitative. The research is focused on exploring meanings, perceptions, and experiences about interpreter skills. The design selected is a case study because it allows an in-depth analysis of a specific context, in this situation the Foreign Languages Teaching program and its students. That is why the instruments used will be semi-structured interviews, observations, field notes, and reflective journals, with the intention of collecting detailed information about participants’ experiences, communicative practices, and perceptions. Intentional sampling will be used to choose the sample, including students of Intensive Practice III of the Bachelor's Degree in Foreign Languages Teaching program. Finally, the process to examine the data will include coding to identify patterns and themes, cataloging the necessary skills, and interpreting participants’ outlooks. The findings will allow the identification of primary principles.
